🏠 Minneapolis & Minnesota Homebuyer Programs & Local Rules ▼
🏡
Homestead Exemption
Lower tax rate for owner-occupied duplex units
▼

When you occupy one unit of a duplex, the owner-occupied portion qualifies for the 1.00% homestead class rate (first $500K of value). The rental unit is assessed at the higher 1.25% non-homestead rate.

This split can save $200–$400+ per year. File a Homestead Application with Hennepin County Assessor by December 31 to receive the benefit the following tax year. Call 612-348-3046 for help.

💰
MN Housing Start Up Loan
Up to $18,000 — deferred, 0% interest DPA
▼

Minnesota Housing's Start Up program offers a deferred second mortgage of up to $18,000 for down payment and closing costs. The loan is interest-free and repayment is deferred until sale, refinance, or the home is no longer a primary residence.

Limits: Max purchase price $310,000 in the Twin Cities metro · Min credit score 640 · Income limits apply · Requires a homebuyer education course. See mnhousing.gov.

🏙️
Minneapolis Homes Access DPA
Up to $20,000 for Minneapolis primary residences
▼

The Minneapolis Homes: Access program offers up to $20,000 in down payment assistance for buyers purchasing a primary residence within Minneapolis city limits.

Income limits and application requirements apply. Contact Minneapolis CPED or visit minneapolismn.gov/housing for current details and to confirm duplex eligibility.

🌱
First-Generation Homebuyer Loan
Up to $35,000 — partially forgivable over 20 years
▼

MN Housing's First Generation Homebuyer Loan provides up to $35,000 for buyers whose parents never owned a home (or lost one to foreclosure). Must be paired with a Start Up first mortgage.

Forgiveness: 50% forgiven after 10 years of owner-occupancy; remaining 50% after 20 years. Deferred, 0% interest. Limited slots — first-come, first-served. See mnhousing.gov.

📋
Rental License Exemption
Owner-occupants generally exempt from licensing
▼

Minneapolis requires a rental license for landlords who don't live on the property. If you occupy one unit as your primary residence, you are generally exempt — a meaningful operational simplification.

If you ever vacate and rent both units, a license is required (~$450 at ownership change + annual renewal by March 1). New 2025 rules: mandatory disclosures before lease signing and 30-day pre-eviction notice.

📈
Rent Stabilization Status
No rent control currently in effect (as of 2026)
▼

Minneapolis voters approved a charter amendment in 2021 giving the City Council authority to regulate rents, but no ordinance has been enacted as of early 2026. A proposal capping annual increases at ~3% has been discussed but not passed.

Note: St. Paul has a separate 3% annual cap — Minneapolis does not. You can currently raise rent at lease renewal. Monitor minneapolismn.gov/housing for updates.

Program details, income limits, and rates change frequently. Verify current eligibility at mnhousing.gov and minneapolismn.gov before applying. This calculator is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial, tax, or legal advice.
